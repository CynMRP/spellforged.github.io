About

PROJECT Spellforged is an upcoming, unique Minecraft RPG experience for Java Edition. It features difficult encounters, satisfying combat, and an immersive world to adventure within.

We are determined to host and deliver an incredible Minecraft RPG that all types of players can adventure in. Whether you love to feel the thrill of combat, or explore the world with your friends to find loot and adventure, PROJECT Spellforged will have something in store for everyone to enjoy.

Classes & Abilities

PROJECT Spellforged offers 5 Classes. While they offer their standard playstyles, all Classes can branch out to be something more. Each class has dozens of Abilities and Passives to choose from, allowing you to decide how you want to play your class.

Classes are specialized in 3 Weapon Types, allowing them to use the Weapon Skills and Class-Weapon Skills from those weapons. However, all classes can melee attack with all weapons , meaning Warriors can use a Bow to finish off fleeing targets.

The following demonstrations have been recorded with low-level classes in order to showcase the power of your class even when you are just beginning your journey. And as you explore, you will only get access to greater magics...

Intense Combat

Combat is a primary focus in PROJECT Spellforged. Our Combat System is diverse and complex, ranging from simple attacks such as firing magic bolts, to dual-wielding Swords and comboing together Abilities to eliminate your foes.

The F System - Efficient Ability Casting

Casting Abilities: [Swap Key] + [Key Bind]

With over 100 Abilities and Passives combined in PROJECT Spellforged, casting those Abilities needs to be fluid, and needs a good amount of Ability Slots for you to assign them to.

PROJECT Spellforged allows you to bind usable Abilities in 8 Ability Slots, and cast them in rapid succession using a system we've named The F-System. This system allows you to use the Swap Key (Usually keybound to F) to prime an ability cast, then press a respective keybind to activate the skill. Passives do not take up an Ability Slot.

With this system and the proper Game Options toggled on, casting all of your abilities is 2 key inputs away. The available inputs you have are as followed:

Ability Slot # Key Combination Ability Slot # Key Combination
1 Swap Key + Left Click 5 Swap Key + Sneak + Left Click
2 Swap Key + Right Click 6 Swap Key + Sneak + Right Click
3 Swap Key + Drop Key 7 Swap Key + Sneak + Drop Key
4 Swap Key + Swap Key 8 Swap Key + Sneak + Swap Key
Dual-Wielding & Shields - The Offhand Slot Reborn

Equipping an Offhand Item: Place it in your Offhand Slot manually via your Inventory.

Offhanding in Minecraft is an underappreciated mechanic. We intend to make it both useful and important by implementing Dual-Wielding and Shields into PROJECT Spellforged.

Dual-Wielding allows you to attack with 2 weapons in both your Mainhand and your Offhand. You can swing with your Offhand by Right Clicking, and certain Game Options allow you to switch that up if you're not a fan of it. This allows you to experience melee combat in a unique way, and makes certain weapons such as Daggers - weak in damage but swift in speed - especially viable.

Shields are present in PROJECT Spellforged, remade in a way to fit a proper RPG. Use Shields to defend against powerful attacks, or to push back with special Shield Skills that can be used to counter these attacks. Shields are especially good for a Tanking role in your party, so it is advised for you to use one if you intend to hold the line within your Party.

Sprint Dashing - Combat Movement Enhanced

Using Dash: Start Sprinting

Movement can be a large reason of why combat is fun and intense. Some enemy attacks may need both speed and reaction time in order to evade them.

PROJECT Spellforged has implemented a simple, yet useful mechanic called Sprint Dashing. This will give you a large, quick-fading burst of speed when you begin sprinting. This allows you to dodge attacks faster and in a stylized manner.

Sprint Dashing isn't unlimited though, and has a short cooldown attached to it. Be sure you time it wisely, otherwise you may find yourself taking a hit you won't be able to withstand...

Expansive Item System

Items and their strengths are important in PROJECT Spellforged. Many items you will find in the world have a use or purpose besides being sold to a vendor for money.

Accessories - The Third Equipment Type

Accessory Types: Trinkets, Gemstones, Rings, Charms

While Weapons grant you damage output and Armor improves your defenses and Attributes, Accessories are a third equipment type that will allow you to enhance your character in a different style.

Accessories give you unique Stats not typically found on other equipment types, such as Mana Regen, Critical Strike Chance, and Threat Generation. These stats can affect your playstyle depending on what you wear.

When exploring the world, be sure to look out for these kinds of items. Accessories can be crucial to your playstyle depending on what you need the most.


Item Enhancements - Customize your Equipment

Enhancement Styles: Refinements, Reforges, Enchantments

You will not only find normal Equipment in the world, but also items and places that can be used to enhance them, such as Refinement Materials or Enchantment Altars. The power that these can give to your equipment can effectively change or enhance how you play.

  • Refinements upgrade your equipment's base stats directly. They can improve a Weapon's Damage, an Armor piece's defensive stats, or an Accessory's stat. Equipment can be refined multiple times at the cost of either Refinement Materials, or using other Equipment.
  • Reforges grant your equipment additional sub-stats, allowing you to customize what attributes it will grant you. Weapons, Equipment, and Accessories can be reforged.
  • Enchantments are unique empowerments of your equipment. Rather than improving its stats, Enchantments grant you special abilities while you wear the equipment that has them. You can obtain them by finding Enchantment Altars out in the world, and offering specific items to enchant. Equipment can only have 1 Enchantment on them at a time.

Using Equipment - Nonrestrictive System

What you can use: Everything!

As you fill up your Bank, you will find many types of Equipment and Weapons. PROJECT Spellforged has very few restrictions on equipment usage, allowing you to create diverse item builds.

All classes can melee attack with all Weapon Types and equip all types of armor. Utilize Bows as a Warrior to pick off enemies from afar, or save yourself a Dagger as a Caster for your last resort.

There are only 2 kinds of restrictions to wearing equipment: Level Requirement and Equipment Weight.

  • All Equipment has a Level Requirement attached to them. Not meeting this requirement will prevent you from using the equipment, and you will not gain any stats from them.
  • Equipment Weight is a flexible system where Weapons and Armor have a Weight value attached to them, and each class has a preset Weapon and Armor Weight maximum. These values add on whenever you are using them, and going over the capacity of either your Weapon Weight or Armor Weight will invoke penalties. However, they don't prevent you from wielding 2 Greatswords...
